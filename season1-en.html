Season 1 — The Dreamer

I decided to ask only about the reposts. She told me not to pay attention to it, but it's really hard not to pay attention when even my recommendations show her reposts.

We wanted to go buy something else, but after standing for a few seconds in front of the glass doors, we realized the store was already closed.

It was time to say goodbye. We shook hands and parted near the same shopping center where we had actually started our walk. And at that very moment, she sent me a huge list of completely different questions. Ranging from "what's your favorite color?" to "what makes you happy?" Within two hours, I had answered all of them. And then she said she would prepare a surprise.

One weekend, Elya didn't come online for a whole day for some reason; I tried my hardest to reach her. When she finally replied, she said she was feeling bad. I asked why, but she didn't want to tell, and I didn't insist. Instead, I suggested we just watch a movie to relax. She agreed with surprise. I wanted us to watch Fight Club. It was my favorite movie. But she didn't want to watch it and kept dodging. In the end, we didn't watch it.

It took me about an hour to get to her place, and on top of that, as luck would have it, my dad needed my help that day. I had to refuse him because he hadn't mentioned it in the morning — on the day I was supposed to go to Elya's. I had to lie and say I was going out with friends. Why did I say that? Because I was afraid that things might go wrong, and to avoid jinxing it — I lied.

That day, we went with school to another, neighboring city. We were walking down the street when suddenly sirens and phones went off. It was 12:00. I even filmed it. It turned out to be a test of emergency alerts. Right after school, I went home, and then to Elya's.

Even after I reached her place by bus, I still had to walk about a kilometer from the stop. At that moment, I thought it was worth it. As I approached her house, she was already waiting for me. We said hello and went into the two-story house. That same aunt greeted us, and then we went into Elya's room. She warned me that people might come in from time to time because Elya's parents had guests. We sat down on the couch and watched one and a half movies — first "UglyDolls" and then "Sharks in Paris". She bought energy drinks and chips. She bought me a Burn, and for herself — a white Monster. I was so nervous that I downed three Burns. After reading all of that, I hugged her and didn't want to let go; my mind just couldn't comprehend it. I couldn't even imagine anything like that, especially that it was all for me. I got dressed, and she went to see me off. We walked halfway, I hugged her again, and we said goodbye. I was on cloud nine. I walked the remaining half and got on the bus. When I got home, I was happier than ever. It was the first time a girl had given me so much attention and care; I remembered that.

The rest of the time, we kept talking, and everything seemed fine. But what's life without unexpected surprises? One day, I found out that she had a boyfriend, Kirill. But this guy lived in another country; they had never even seen each other. And after I found out, I made a huge mistake: I came across some video on TikTok related to love, and I decided to write and share my situation.

I don't know who did it, I don't know why, and I don't know how, but some random, unknown person sent Elya a private message with that comment of mine where I had told what happened to me. Elya read my comment and didn't say anything to me about it at first. But then she told me. We talked it over and agreed to remain friends. Right here, I made another mistake: I decided to continue showing sympathy and hitting on her in every way. Because I firmly believed and hoped that she would be with me — the one who was real, who was physically nearby, and not the one thousands of kilometers away. On top of that, she said she would soon move. 200 km away from here. Anyway, we still decided to meet one last time.

We met again, the third time, on June 30th. But this time it was in my city. We met at my favorite shopping center, near my school. She bought a Red Bull there, and we went for a walk. We talked about various things, but not that conversation about relationships.

Near my house, there was a small park that we soon entered. We sat on the swings and showed each other funny TikTok videos. When we got bored, I asked my friend Fluffy to come over. He came, and the three of us went for a walk. We passed by the coast and reached my school, where I was supposed to study the next school year. Later, we climbed a cliff that overlooked my entire neighborhood, and Fluffy stepped away from us, leaving Elya and me alone. Then she admitted she was tired of a long-distance relationship; she said she needed physical touch and comfort. I listened and thought I had a chance, thought she would choose me. I believed in that, in hope. Just as our conversation was ending, Fluffy came back. He said he needed to get his bike, which he had ridden to meet us. We told him we'd wait for him at the shopping center — exactly where we had started. Fluffy went down, and we went up. By coincidence, we were alone again. That's when she told me what annoyed her about that relationship. Or rather, about long-distance relationships. Watching the movement of her lips and her hair blowing in the wind, I couldn't look away. Her words were like balm to my soul, and hope fed on them. Then I expressed my point of view on the matter. I said that a relationship where abuse rules is not worth it. I tried to make her see that she needed to pay attention to reality, not an illusion.

While we were talking, we had already reached my other school — the one where I studied when I was texting her via Telegram or TikTok. I showed her the place where I used to text her (the school backyard), and then we headed to that same shopping center where Fluffy was already waiting for us. We sat and talked for about ten more minutes, and then we all said goodbye to each other. Fluffy knew about Elya even before the walk. You see, one Friday after Felles, Fluffy, Artem, and I were discussing Elya (Artem is my friend). They claimed that building a relationship with her was not an option, at least because she was moving very soon. Driving 200 km to meet would have been somewhat irrational.

After the walk, I got on the bus, and she immediately texted me from her second account. She said Kirill had access to her main account and was reading our correspondence, and that he was jealous. Elya said that his jealousy had already gotten on her nerves, so she texted me from the second account. If you think about it, Kirill's jealousy is quite justified, but in any case, Elya set her priorities the way she wanted. We texted all night — or rather, she was breaking up with him, and I was supporting her. Elya took a long time to make this decision; she kept wanting to change her mind, but finally, she sent him a huge message in which she apologized a thousand times for being the first to confess and that the desire to break up also came from her. At that time, Kirill was trying to keep control and wanted to discuss everything. Meanwhile, Elya was crying, and I was comforting her in every possible way. Of course, I tried to stay in touch the whole time and said I would come to her in the morning to calm her down and say goodbye. Elya was supposed to move to another city 200 kilometers away; I considered options to come, but I didn't think about it seriously. As it turned out later, she hadn't broken up with Kirill — she had just started lying. Later, she herself admitted that she hadn't broken up with him. As I already said, I felt she was leading me by the nose, but I tried to ignore it. And after her confession, I was very upset. I still had feelings for her. Nevertheless, I kept my word and decided to go to her, to her house.

That night I didn't sleep; in the morning, I went to the store, took a free coffee at Kiwi, and went to her place.

I arrived at 8, but I didn't sit right by her house — I found a log near the mailboxes and sat there. There were no benches, nor sidewalks. Her house was in the middle of nowhere — just fields and a few houses. From the moment we finished that conversation, she didn't reply; she didn't even come online, even though I wrote more than once.

Finally, at 3 p.m., as I was already waiting for the bus, she got in touch. She said she was very sorry, that I should have called instead of walking around all that time. She also said she wouldn't survive without me (though that was an obvious lie to keep me on a leash) and that I was a lifeline for her. After that, I was happy at first, but then I realized that a "lifeline" is by no means a partner. But the most interesting thing of all she said was that she would stop talking to Kirill. What do you think happened? Right! — A lie.

A few days later, I couldn't take it anymore and said so much on emotions (and even tried to be gentle so as not to offend too much) that even she couldn't stand it and wrote a huge message apologizing and saying she was very sorry that I came across a person like her. And of course, Kirill got involved. He started texting me privately, saying I shouldn't have finished her off. After reading Elya's and Kirill's messages, something snapped in me.

I started calling and texting her wherever I could. Naturally, she didn't pick up and, of course, didn't reply. In the end, she deleted her Telegram accounts (and mind you, one of them had a premium subscription). It was a very hard day for me. Half the night, I tried to reach her or somehow get in touch. When I had more or less calmed down, I texted her via SMS. Oh, miracle! There she finally replied.

I asked, no, I begged her to continue our communication again; I also said I wasn't mad about everything she had done and added that I didn't care about her lies. In the end, I can only say that my persistence was my own undoing. I constantly wrote to her, hoped, believed, but she didn't choose me. What she did at the end — ghosting — I understood how painful that is. When the ignoring and cold replies first began, I couldn't do anything; I couldn't eat, couldn't drink, couldn't even get out of bed. I fell into a monstrous depression from which I couldn't escape for several months. But I understood that this experience was important for me; I had to go through it so that in the future I wouldn't repeat the same mistakes. Thanks to this destructive force, I can start with a clean slate, although I still want to rest.

Elya always considered me just a friend, or only someone who could comfort in a difficult time. And I, like a fool, believed that friendship could grow into a relationship, but the sad truth is that you don't date friends.

She would never have considered me a potential partner for a relationship. At the very least, because she always called me "Bro" or "Lifeline". And at most — her actions. Maybe because of this, I developed a trigger for that word "bro", in its various forms.

I made mistakes, but I learned my lesson and won't make them again.
