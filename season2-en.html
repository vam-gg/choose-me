Season 2 — One-Man Show

Arina. I never thought I'd think about her again. Everything I'm about to say won't be entirely accurate, but the essence will be the same. Let's begin.

After my "tragedy" on August 1st (which I've already told you about), changes + depression began. That same August 1st, I watched some video by "Marazm" about voice chat (a random voice chat roulette on Telegram). I got curious and decided to join it — at that time, I was going to the pull-up bars and had internet. In that same voice chat, I found a friend — Denchik. I told him what had happened to me, and he gladly supported me and even shared a similar story from his own life. Remembering Nectomi, I decided to go there and chat (Nectomi is also a voice chat — that is, a voice chat roulette).

It was August 5th. After my first gym workout, I came home and plopped down on the couch, and right then I went into Nectomi. Less than an hour later, some guy in that same Nectomi suggested I join a Telegram group where he and others, gathered from Nectomi like me, were hanging out. I joined that group and immediately saw a message from some guy. He asked basic things and acted a bit younger than his age (he said he was 18 but talked to me as if he were 14). He introduced himself as "Vadim", and I introduced myself as Andrey, respectively. At the end of our conversation, he mentioned that at that moment his sister, Arina, was pissing him off because she had PMS. I explained that it happens with girls and it's nothing out of the ordinary. Then he wrote:

— Want me to sell her to you?

— For how much? — I replied;

— For free. — he answered without hesitation;

— A bit pricey, but I'll buy. — I joked.

After that, he threw me her username. I was in a relatively good mood, so I started the conversation with a joke:

— Bonjour. — I wrote;

— What? — she replied immediately, as if she'd been waiting;

— That's "hello" in French. — I clarified.

From that moment, we started talking. Everything seemed fine, but suddenly I found out that she lived not far from Khabarovsk (that is, not far from Vladivostok — the Far East, closer to Japan). I did the math, and it turned out we had an 8-hour time difference. To make it clearer — when I went to bed, she was getting up for school. And it also turned out she was older than me by exactly one day (my birthday is September 16th, hers is the 15th) — that hooked me even more. From that day on, I couldn't put my phone down because we talked very often. We even called each other repeatedly. For example, in the morning I would go outside and walk along the embankment or near free Wi‑Fi. Precisely because of this time difference, I messed up my sleep. I talked with her at night, kept waking up constantly, and it really affected both my physical and mental health. Because she lived in a village, the internet wasn't always good, so calls often lagged, but still we kept talking. And then, I started to feel something — that feeling could be described as falling in love. I confessed on August 13th, yes, just a week after we met. To my confession, she replied:

— Why is it always "before friendship"? — she wrote in bewilderment;

— With us — yes. — I replied;

— ... I like you too. — she answered a couple of minutes later.

At that moment, I was overjoyed, thinking I had actually entered a relationship. But the joy didn't last long. From that point, the first act of this theater began.

One day, a couple of days after our mutual confession, some Vlad Primakov wrote to me and started saying something about Arina being his girlfriend. The most interesting thing happened right after that conversation. She texts me, saying that apparently there's nothing between us, and all those confessions were supposedly written by her sister. Turns out she was in a relationship with this Vlad. She told this to me and him, hoping we'd believe it, but no. In the end, Vlad blocked her, but I didn't.

At that time, I went on a trip again — to a small town called "Bø", basically into the mountains, for the weekend. By the way, all that time I was also talking with Vadim, that is, Arina's brother. After I told Vlad about Vadim, he told me that Vadim was indeed her brother — but one who had hanged himself a couple of years ago. You might ask: how was I talking to a corpse? And I'll answer — as it turned out, the Vadim I was talking to was not Vadim, but Matvey. So from the very beginning, he had introduced himself with a made-up name. But I decided to forgive that lie — to this "Vadim", or rather, Matvey. And I continued talking to Matvey. In all of this, I noticed one unusual pattern: whenever I quarreled with Arina, I would write about it to Matvey. A couple of minutes later, somehow, Matvey would calm Arina down every time, and accordingly, she would forgive me, and we'd continue talking. It's also important that Arina blocked me after every quarrel (and there were many).

The first few times she blocked me, I thought, "That's it! It's over! We've really screwed up..." because I was basing it on my recent past experience with Elya. There was another significant moment: after Vlad dumped her, she called me on the phone and genuinely cried, saying she couldn't live without him, of course she said she loved him, and blamed me for not believing her. After their breakup, she accused me of ruining her life. And I was calming her down and acted like a pillow you can cry into.

I decided to wait for the moment when everything settled and confess my feelings to her again. After all, I thought she was the one and I had a chance. And let me remind you, my sleep schedule was completely wrecked, so I wasn't really thinking straight.

I confessed my feelings to her again on August 31st. To my huge surprise, she reciprocated, and this time it was definitely for real... Or was it?

In addition to the usual text confession, I sent her a few Telegram gifts — for stars that you can buy with real money.

That same evening, overjoyed, I told my parents about her. But they said that having this pseudo-relationship wasn't a very good idea, plus I was already thinking about a plan to visit her when I turned 16. But my parents didn't appreciate any of that.

By the way, the so-called "happiness" didn't last long. Around September 5th, she told me that her classmate had been hitting on her, claiming that they were in a relationship. I didn't really attach any importance to it. But soon, some guy wrote to me and introduced himself as that very classmate (his name was Seryozha). He said that he and she were in a relationship. He also said he had broken up with her because he found out she was dating me. He said they had kissed, hugged, joked around, and done everything couples do. Relying on my past experience with Vlad, I believed Arina again. When I told her that this Seryozha had written to me, she immediately asked — no, ordered — me to block him. And since I trust my girlfriend, I blocked Seryozha.

After a while, my conscience and a sense of foreboding started gnawing at me, and I decided to unblock him, at least because I felt awkward before him. He literally hadn't written anything "mystical".

By the way, aside from Arina and my online life, at school I was always without a smile — because of what I was going through with Elya. I mention this because, in September, when I was, as always, without a smile, with a face of tiredness and resignation to all situations, she appeared — Masha (my classmate from a parallel class, but when we went to extra Norwegian lessons, me, Masha, and a couple of other kids were in the same class). Masha came up and asked, "Why aren't you smiling?" I just nodded and that was it. And from that moment, a lot changed. Perhaps I even unconsciously started to pay attention to her. We said hello, but in fact, we didn't really talk. That's Masha for you.

September 15th, Arina's birthday. I gave her another Telegram gift. But for my own birthday, I received a special gift: she told me she didn't feel anything anymore. Moreover, she said:

— No, it's my fault, those words "I love you" and all that, I also want to say it to you but, damn it, I've got nothing, I'm scared too, and when I replied to you offhandedly, I felt pain, and when I blocked you, also pain; when I'm in pain, that's what I do, and you just delete my attention, and I behave like a bitch, sorry that I'm so bad, but really, you love me, you're suffering, trying to get mutual feelings, and I can't reciprocate, if I date you without feelings, it would be like pity, and it's not your fault that you're depressed, it's Elya who messed you up and I finished you off, but I didn't know it would turn out like this, I understand you perfectly, but I'm just... a bitch. — she said, seemingly even sincerely;

— I don't understand, what makes you think someone messed me up? — I still didn't understand what was really going on at that moment.

In the end, on September 16th, we broke up and decided to remain friends. By the way, a few times her sister interfered in our relationship — because, according to Arina, she had access to her Telegram account. This sister would write to me on Arina's behalf, saying she wanted to break up or worse. As Arina said, her sister's name was Katya.

By the way, later something happened that left me in huge shock. It turned out that Katya was actually Seryozha's sister, and to Arina, she was just her best friend. Of course, all along I suspected that her brother Matvey didn't exist, and I thought she was writing to me from his account to find out what I was feeling. One of the clues was that whenever I wrote something specifically to Matvey, Arina somehow immediately found out. I also often wondered: why were Matvey and Arina always, always together? Like, literally always — why? One of my main suspicions arose when Matvey wrote something, and Arina went offline. By the end of September, I had started to talk normally with Seryozha. Because he was the one who told me that Katya was his sister. I began to trust him more than Arina. And besides, he didn't reek of lies, and he talked like a normal person.

An interesting transitional moment was when Arina found out about Elya (through my main account, where her gifts were). Arina constantly started dragging Elya into every quarrel, even when the conversation was about something completely different. The absurdity was that she knew almost nothing about Elya. She only knew that we used to talk and that we stopped talking on her initiative. But this dialogue kept happening:

— Why do you believe Seryozha and not me? — she says;

— He has more proof — I replied;

— Oh yeah? Well then, fuck off to your little Elya. — she was deliberately hitting a sore spot;

— What does she have to do with this? — I asked;

— Mm. — that damn "Mm" almost always ended our conversation.

Soon I got completely fed up with all of it — the fact that Katya kept butting into our private messages, Arina's lies, dragging Elya into everything — so I decided to end communication with Arina for good. I was planning to do it at the end of September, but it happened in mid-October. Our last conversation went like this:

— Well, okay, maybe we'll talk again sometime. — I said;

— Hmm, probably not ever. — she replied.

About this, I also made a post in my Telegram channel, where I wrote: "Life sometimes throws the same scenario at us so we can analyze our mistakes and draw the appropriate conclusions. The first time, I acted stupidly and paid for it later. This time, I won't let that happen. If you look at my behavior from a psychological perspective, I was looking for salvation in her, seeking shelter from thoughts about what happened to me before August. You know, at some point, everything becomes unimportant. Who's right, who's wrong. Just fatigue remains... And a light sadness after everything that's happened." Above the text, I attached a screenshot showing July 18th and October 18th — me ending communication. The first time with Elya, the second with Arina. Meanwhile, I was on good terms with Seryozha and still am to this day. He turned out to be the most adequate character in this whole theater.

Later it turned out that Katya never had access to Arina's Telegram account. Arina was just brazenly riding on my feelings, like on her tenth boyfriend's dick. She often played the pity card and always made me out to be the guilty one. When we were together, Arina constantly talked to her ex-boyfriend Vlad, even though I repeatedly asked her not to talk to him.

There was another very unpleasant moment: after she quarreled with Katya, I tried to talk to her, and she ignored me. But when she finally replied, she said she had been talking to Vlad and complaining to him — quoting: about the whole world. And when I asked why she didn't want to talk to me about what was bothering her, she said she simply didn't want to, and that hurt me. But the most interesting thing happened a month later: she said she had just been joking and hadn't talked to him at that time.

It's no surprise that even to this day, she talks to him and gives him the gifts I gave her, and so on.

In this text, I didn't say that I was in love with her, because I didn't love her either. I just wanted to find a little hideaway so I wouldn't be in that morally destroyed state.

An interesting fact: she loved it when I wrote something to her, and she would fall asleep to it.

* * *

Now that Arina has faded into the background, or more precisely, disappeared from my life, from mid-October to November I simply watched "her" from a distance. I looked at "her" and "admired her". Once, we even met eyes; at that moment, as if everything around and behind her dissolved. There was only her. All sounds seemed to disappear, just like in the movies, haha. And when I realized she had asked something, I immediately came to my senses and asked again.

As you can see, such things happen in life.

(This is a work of fiction. Any coincidences are purely coincidental)
