Season 3 — Unforgotten Old

Elya, Arina, and some Masha — how have I not gone crazy yet? But let's start with the fact that all the time I was talking to Arina, Elya was like a shadow. But on September 16th, everything changed. Elya resurfaced. She posted in her Telegram channel that she was watching Fight Club. Yes, the one I told her about a long time ago. Then the next day she published another post — she was at the same spot where I had sat with her, waiting for my pants to dry, right by the water. That same day she posted a photo of her watching Dexter (the series I always talked to her about); I immediately realized she was hinting at me.

I didn't hesitate and started putting a "😨" emoji on every post in her channel. When she noticed, she immediately understood it was me and joked, "Andrey was popular even in the 20th century." On September 24th, I wished Elya a happy birthday (from my other account) and she replied "thank you so much" and didn't block me. Then I noticed a very interesting detail — since September 14th, her boyfriend hadn't been online.

As I said, sometime at the beginning of October, she appeared: Masha. At first I didn't notice her, but then she started showing some strange signs of attention. Right after that, I began to watch what she was doing and where she was. It was purely out of curiosity, but then I stepped back and said to myself:

— Why am I watching? I understand that nothing will ever happen with her. I'm just wasting time and nerves, giving myself empty hopes.

At that same moment, a line from a song played in my head:

— ...We're bored to live, and that's why there are so many problems. — (from the song "Ritmy Okon" by Vasya Oblomov)

— That explains everything. — I replied to myself.

After that, I stopped watching.

To my surprise, after I stopped watching, Masha started showing signs of attention again. For example, when she asked me after class in a different room during the break what I was reading, and added, "Did you get your stuff from that classroom?" — when she could have not asked. Besides that, a couple of days later the following situation happened: I was standing during a break and suddenly felt like something was about to happen, that I just needed to stand there and do nothing for it to happen. Right that minute, Masha comes up, sits on the chair next to me, and starts a conversation:

— Waiting for someone?

— Nah, just standing here. How are you? — I replied and asked;

— Not so great — she answered vaguely;

— Why?

— Well, because we're at school, what did you think? Haha, what are you doing after school? — she said jokingly;

— Gotta go with my parents to another school for a meeting. And you? — I said, because I really did have to go to a meeting;

— I'm going to practice — to dance. — she said;

— Hah, yeah, I knew.

— Really? How? — she said, surprised and questioning;

— From Instagram. — a couple of days earlier I had found her on Insta and followed her. Then I added, — Want some gum?

She agreed with a smile. Right after that, a teacher came up and they had a short conversation about dancing. She seemed to deliberately mention the days she goes to dance. The most surprising thing was when she said something like: "Why, oh why, doesn't anyone want to come watch my practice?" Maybe it was a hint, but I either didn't get it or didn't want to get it. After she talked to the teacher, we sat a little longer, then she stood up and said she was going downstairs, and left. And I continued standing there.

While watching Masha, I didn't forget about Elya. Throughout this whole story with Arina and Masha, I kept an eye on Elya through her Telegram channel. At the beginning of October, she published a not-so-positive post in her channel; I thought for a long time whether I should write anything at all, and in the end I decided to support her. I sent her a couple of encouraging private messages. A week passed, and she still hadn't replied — hadn't even read them. When I realized it was pointless, I deleted the messages. But I wasn't upset because somewhere towards the end of October, she posted in her channel a link allowing anyone to send her an anonymous message. I couldn't pass up that opportunity and sent my message:

— Do you know who's putting those emojis? — I wrote anonymously;

— Andrey, of course. — she replied, as if guessing.

I didn't hesitate and, on adrenaline, wrote in the comments:

— What do I have to do with it?

And off it went. Her friend immediately started teasing me in every possible way and writing all sorts of insults, but instead of latching onto it and escalating the conflict, I simply started mocking everything she said and giving soft comebacks. In turn, Elya tried to calm us down. And when I realized I couldn't handle it anymore, I asked for help from Artem (my friend who always listened to me about Elya) and Seryozha, who had helped me escape Arina's lies. Both of them helped me a lot. When everything devolved into a joke, or rather into a more or less calm pace, the topic of Dexter came up.

— When you posted about Dexter, Artem and I were shocked. — I said.

— Haha, why? — Elya replied in bewilderment.

— DM. — that was my plan to get unblocked, and it worked.

Here's what happened in DMs:

— He and I were sitting there, freaking out about that Fight Club post, and then right at that moment you posted about Dexter. He looked at me and said something like "damn, Andrey," and I sent a sticker. — I started explaining our surprise.

— The question was about the reason for our surprise, actually. — she said;

— Because it was unexpected. Plus, I used to ramble on about it all the time. We were freaking out about Fight Club because, again, I was always telling you about it. And then out of nowhere, you're watching it. — I tried to gather my thoughts as quickly as possible;

— There you go, you should've started with that — answering my question, everything else is secondary. Well, if you're wondering why I decided to watch it — I was just bored, that's all. There's been nothing decent lately, so I remembered and watched it. — she said in response.

When the topic turned to movies, I asked her if she liked Fight Club, and she said yes. She added that she liked it more than Dexter. A couple of days later, one evening I talked with Elya for 4 hours before bed. I ended up falling asleep at half past three in the morning. The funny thing is, I realized that while her boyfriend wasn't online (his laptop had broken), I was like a spare, probably. Besides, I wasn't feeling anything; my heart was beating calmly, and I got the sense that we were talking just like the first time, or like former friends, without any continuation of anything. It's even a bit sad because I had always craved communication with her, constantly thought about her. And in the end, when I realized nothing was going to happen with her, I simply let go.

About a week later, I broke my phone. I went without it for about two weeks until they bought me a new one. During that time, Elya didn't write to me once.

Let me remind you that when Elya and I started talking again, everything was already different. I was different, the time was different, and that guy (Kirill) was basically gone. I decided to present myself in the best light. Compared to summer, I had become more rugged. The problem arose when I started falling for her again. We often hung out in voice chats or on TikTok streams, talked, laughed. I also often quarreled with guys on the stream when they said something about Elya.

One of them was her friend, but unlike the other random guys, I rarely reacted to him. His name was Vlad; he was the quietest on the stream. But one day, everything changed.

As usual on a Friday, I went to Felles (Fellesverket / Felles — that's the place I often go to on Fridays. It's a building where me and others, mostly Russian-speaking, come from different cities to play pool, listen to music in the studio, play PlayStation, and much more. Usually pizza, chips, fruit, and various drinks are served as food. Sometimes events like Mafia and various activities are held there). There I can calmly talk with friends and hang out on streams.

That very Friday, I was sitting on a stream in Felles. Scarlett (Milana, Elya's friend with whom I got along well), Vlad, and another person were there. At first, Vlad, as always, didn't show any activity and only occasionally said something. When I was talking to some guy, I made a gay joke in the direction of that guy and Vlad. Everyone understood we were joking, so everyone laughed — except Vlad.

— Of course, you, Max, and Vladik are my little cuties.

— The last person who said that to me was talking without teeth. — Vlad replied.

After that response, Elya and that friend laughed, and I decided to continue the joke and simply replied: "don't be offended, cutie." He just snorted.

Before this, Vlad had already appeared in conversation in relation to me. Specifically, when I was quarreling with some guy once again, Vlad would say "don't fight, girls" — I hadn't reacted to him until the "cutie" moment. The mess started closer to night; around half past nine in the evening, they started playing Truth or Dare. In this game, they often asked questions or assigned dares in a lewd context. But since I was heading home, I wasn't sitting there on the stream and could only guess what was happening. When I came back to the stream, there was absolutely nothing left of a normal conversation. Only lust remained. Then they moved from the stream to a voice chat on Telegram (by then only Elya, Elya's friend (Scarlett/Milana), Vlad, and I were there).

Because it was night and I had arrived home, I couldn't properly stay on the stream and only popped in occasionally. They talked for half the night, but then Scarlett and I went to sleep. At that point, they were left one-on-one. It turned out that I could no longer control them. Naturally, I wasn't happy about that.

Sometime before dawn, I woke up and went back into that voice chat and asked what was happening and what they were doing. Elya, with a light laugh, said it was private. That crushed me even more. Right after those words, I left the voice chat. A few days passed; I restrained myself from going into that voice chat, and soon I left it altogether. I did it because I simply needed to let Elya go. Part of me understood that Elya would very soon enter a relationship with Vlad. My intuition didn't let me down. After a short while, Elya chose Vlad.

The situation was exacerbated by the fact that Vlad also lived in another country; he was already 18 (and Elya, mind you, was 15). When my friends told me that Vlad was just her friend, it comforted me. How wrong they were; the most hurtful part came when I saw that in her Telegram bio she had written "There's a beloved 'V' — others fuck off." Right then, all my suspicions came true. Soon they even had a TikTok streak. The hurt was compounded by the fact that Elya had told me she didn't want to create streaks because you had to constantly take care of them. But somehow they had a streak. Confirmation that they were in a relationship was the emoji status "I [heart] Vlad". After that, I became completely disillusioned with her and made the final decision to let go. Some time later, Elya officially confirmed that she was in a relationship.

So what about Masha, damn it? Here's what happened:

Masha first made her TikTok profile private when she saw I had visited it; of course, I sent a follow request. But then, when she approved the request, a video was posted on TikTok where two of her friends were talking, and one asked the other:

— I think Masha's in love.

— Why? — the other friend asked, and the first one replied —

— Blush.

The camera pans to Masha, and she has artificial blush drawn on her cheeks.

But all that attention towards me didn't last long. There could be a ton of reasons. Starting from the fact that I didn't understand the hints, ending with the fact that I simply became uninteresting to her. And honestly, I didn't really want to start anything with her anyway — because I know what kind of person she is and what her character's like. It ended with Masha starting to ignore me completely, blocked me on TikTok, and even began pretending not to notice my basic "hi" or "bye." But when we accidentally met on the bus during the holidays, she was nervous. Though she wasn't alone — there were two other guys and another girl with her — she still said hello with a forced smile. From that moment on, Masha stopped ignoring me and began to behave calmly. That's definitely a good thing.

Inhale... Exhale... Elya... Again... Her relationship with Vlad didn't last long — just a bit over a month. Then — they broke up, and the most interesting part is that she told me about it herself. And with that, I made a fatal mistake: I changed my decision to "let her go." Ultimately, we started talking often at night. At night we joked, laughed, I was often jealous of her. But I was constantly torn by two personalities. One said she liked me, and the other said it was all in vain and she was just keeping me in the friend zone.

On New Year's Eve, I sent her a gift on Telegram — right at 0:00, and congratulated her via text. I had done the same a year before: on New Year's Eve a year earlier, I had given a gift to a girl I liked at the time; her name was Yulya. Ironically, on this very New Year's Eve, I first celebrated with my parents, but then I noticed my friend Artem, Kira (his girlfriend), and, as it happened, Yulya (she was just Kira's sister). I went up to them, and the four of us watched the fireworks. When things had more or less quieted down, I called Elya. That same New Year's night, we talked on the phone for an hour. And the funny thing is, when I called her, I went behind the house and, without noticing in the dark, stepped on a board with nails. But even so, I didn't end the conversation and continued talking to her. Love till the grave? Or attachment till a lethal outcome?

On the night of January 3rd to 4th, a deep sadness hit me, and I told Elya a little about those two personalities I mentioned earlier, but I didn't directly say what each personality was saying. Elya genuinely supported me; in a nutshell, she said to listen to myself, not to other people's opinions. She said she had been in a similar situation herself. After reading her messages, I finally gathered the courage, and at that very moment (1:25 a.m.), I wrote this:

— If there's a moment for honesty, then here it is, I guess... I like you — not just as a friend. I understand that you most likely see me as a friend, and I respect that. I'm not expecting anything in return and I'm not asking for quick decisions. It was important for me to say this, to be honest with myself.

And she replied that she hadn't expected it, and at 1:40 a.m. she answered:

— What can I tell you at this moment — I can see that your feelings for me haven't changed since those very moments. Honestly, I can't reciprocate, and yes, you're right. I consider you a friend, exclusively a friend. I don't feel any attraction to you, but that doesn't change the fact that once I did feel something for you, but then I lost it. You've become a friend to me, a good friend, a brother. Maybe someday attraction towards you will reappear, but I can't say for sure. Besides, I prefer to be without relationships right now; I'm not made for them. I'm not made to maintain relationships with people, not made to maybe build a family in the future. I'm not made for that. My world is built only on solitude. I'm sorry, please forgive me for rejecting you again. But I truly am not made for relationships — with you or with anyone else. I'm sorry, really, I feel ashamed. But I want everything to be as before — for us to be good friends.

But what surprised me was that she had felt something for me back then, in the summer, when I was supporting her — when she was with Kirill. I agreed to remain friends, but I definitely decided for myself that I would phase out communication. Our conversation ended at 2:33 a.m. Today I was once again convinced that she feels nothing for me. Artem had been right the whole time. He said everything I was doing in relation to Elya was just a pointless waste of time. He was right. Everything she said was only to avoid hurting me. And the fact that, as she said, "I'm not made for relationships — with you or with anyone else" is a hundred percent a lie, if only because literally a couple of days later she was openly saying in her channel that she liked some guy. The same argument applies to her statement about wanting to be without relationships for a while.

The day after my confession, she wrote to me "kill me" and then added context that she had school tomorrow and didn't want to go. Throughout the whole conversation, I was slightly distant. After a while, I ended the conversation and said I was going to sleep. A couple of days later, I joined her friend's TikTok stream. We were calmly chatting, but when Elya joined, I left.

Simply put — I started to distance myself, and that's it. And after some time, she met up with some friend from her group; she took a photo of them hugging and published it as her avatar on her Telegram channel, as her profile picture, and of course also posted in her channel with the caption "new avatar for the tg channel." The funniest part was when her friend asked about that guy. She answered that they were just friends and that she didn't need a relationship. That evening I was at Felles; I decided to lighten the mood in the chat and sent a video clip from Felles.

— Bro, I wanna be with you. — said the guy who had been giving Elya Telegram gifts;

— He's already with me. — Elya replied to that guy;

— Who do you mean? — I asked her;

— You. — Elya answered me;

— We were in the same city a year ago. Ah, bro. — she said sadly;

— What? — I said;

— Jeg savner på dag. — she wrote. From Norwegian, that means "I miss you."

She thought I would reply "Jeg også" (meaning "me too"), but no. I simply reacted to the message with a "👍" emoji.

— Come on, what's with that reaction? So cold. — she objected.

But I didn't care anymore, so I didn't write anything else.

Neither Masha, nor Elya, and certainly not Arina would have ever loved me — that's a fact. For Masha, I'm not her type; Elya wouldn't have loved me because of the distance — she would have fallen for an illusion she herself created. Same with Arina, but in addition, Arina simply has issues with her head.

They all share one and the same pattern. At the very least, they gave attention in doses, and at most, they were all manipulators. It's just that some were unconsciously, and some consciously. For example: Elya — ghosting; Arina — lying for the sake of emotions; and Masha did whatever it took for me to give her maximum attention. Moreover, none of them wanted to take responsibility for any of it.

So there you go. We all make mistakes — some to a lesser degree, some to a greater. But the main thing is to learn the lesson and do everything possible not to repeat those same mistakes.

If you draw the right conclusions from any negative experience, that negative experience will only benefit you.

* * *

I could write something about You, but I still don't know what awaits us... Happiness? Love? Or another circle of hell? I don't know. But I'll say one thing — you've caught my attention.

Maybe I had to go through all of this just to be ready for something truly valuable.

There are no villains here, nor heroes. There are only people and their actions.
